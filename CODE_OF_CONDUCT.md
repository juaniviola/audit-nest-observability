# Code of Conduct

## Our pledge

We want `audit-nest-observability` to be a practical, respectful, and safe
space for everyone who contributes, reviews, reports issues, or participates in
technical discussions.

We value direct feedback, strong technical opinions, and healthy disagreement.
We do not accept harassment, personal attacks, discrimination, or behavior that
makes collaboration unsafe.

## Expected behavior

- Be respectful and constructive, especially when you disagree.
- Focus criticism on ideas, code, architecture, tests, and trade-offs.
- Assume good intent, but accept corrections when impact does not match intent.
- Help newcomers understand the project before expecting perfect contributions.
- Prefer clear examples, reproducible cases, and concrete alternatives.
- Respect maintainers' time by keeping issues and pull requests focused.

## Unacceptable behavior

- Harassment, intimidation, threats, or sustained disruption.
- Insults, personal attacks, trolling, or demeaning language.
- Discrimination based on identity, background, experience level, or beliefs.
- Publishing private information without explicit permission.
- Sexualized language or unwanted sexual attention.
- Repeatedly ignoring maintainer decisions or project boundaries.

## Enforcement

Maintainers may remove comments, close issues or pull requests, block users, or
take any other action they consider necessary to protect the project community.

When possible, maintainers should first explain what behavior crossed the line
and give the person a chance to correct it. Severe or repeated violations may be
handled without warning.

## Reporting

If you experience or observe unacceptable behavior, report it privately to the
project maintainers.

Please include:

- a short description of what happened
- links, screenshots, or references when available
- whether the situation is ongoing
- any safety concerns that require immediate action

Reports will be reviewed with care and handled as confidentially as possible.

## Scope

This code of conduct applies in project spaces, including issues, pull requests,
discussions, releases, documentation, and any community channel associated with
the project.
